Plant Life

A Plant is a living organism that usually grows on land or water by absorbing water and nutrients through its plants. A plant produces other nutrients in its leaves by using solar energy.

  • Why are plants considered to be living things?
  • Plants have roots that grow in the soil
  • They have shoots that grow above the soil
  • Plants have branches that hold leaves, fruits and flowers
  • Plants produce their own food and can also die when there aren't nutrients in the soil
"The goal of any plant is to create a new offspring through seed dispersal. Once a good seed arrives at a place where it can germinate, the lifecycle of that plant is ready to begin again."

Life-cycle of flowering plants

Flowering plants each have variations in the ways they grow, bloom, and reproduce. Flowers have been used as symbols, medicines, ceremonial aids and even decorations worldwide. 

Flowering plants are sometimes used as food. They also produce food that man can eat. The flower of a plant is the reproductive part of that plant. 

The Life cycle of a flowering plant usually starts from the Seed. The seeds come in different shapes and sizes depending on the plant. Seeds are planted in the ground. After some time, the seeds begin to germinate. Under the right conditions (atmosphere and soil), a seedling shoots out of the soil and shows its primary leaves. 

As the plant grows and matures, a flower grows from the stem as a little bud. Eventually, the bud opens up to reveal the flower. Plants share reproductive materials called pollen grains through a process called pollination. Pollination occurs when pollen grains from the male (anther) is transported to the female (stigma). Some plants can self-pollinate, while other plants require insects, wind, rain, birds and other means of transferring pollen grains.

After a successful pollination, the seeds become reproductive. A protective layer covers the seed. This is usually called the fruit. Some seeds are large and fleshy like apples, mangoes and pawpaw. The reproductive seed can now be planted in the soil to grow into a new plant. 

Requirements for Healthy Plant Growth

The quality of plant growth  depends on the type of plant and the environment which they are growing. Listed below are the basic requirements for a healthy plant growth.
Adequate space
Plants need space to grow. Any plant that grows in a crowded environment will not grow well because of competition for water and nutrients from the soil.
Sufficient light
The energy from the sun is very important for plant growth. Any plant that grows in a dark or shaded environment will not be able to prode food in their leaves through photosynthesis.
Sufficient water
Water is very necessary for living things to survive. Plants simply cannot survive in a place without water. 
Atmospheric temperature
The temperature of the atmosphere plays a big role in plant growth. There are specific types of plants that can survive in warm temperatures and others that can only grow in a cold environment. 
Mineral Elements in the Soil.
The soil naturally has mineral elements that plants can absorb through their roots. When there is an adequate amount of minerals in the soil, plants will be able to absorb and grow healthy and strong. 
Type of Soil.
There are three main types of soil. Clay, Loamy and Sandy. The loamy soil is the best soil for plant growth because it is better able to hold minerals and water in the soil for plants to grow and absorb.
