Geography

Highlands and rivers

Lesson 5

Highlands are uplands, elevations or raised lands that are above the normal level of the earth's surface. Examples are mountains, hills and plateaus

Some highlands can be found in Nigeria. massive structures like Zuma rock, Olumo rock, Jos plateau, Shere hills, mount dimlang are highlands that exist in Nigeria. 

The highest point in Nigeria is the "Chappal Waddi" located on Shebshi mountains in Taraba state. 

High points and mountain regions generally have cooler temperatures and climates due to their heights. The higher you go, the cooler the air becomes. You can even see snow at the paramount of some mountains. 

Chappal Waddi

A hill in Nigeria, measuring up to 2,419meters, is the country's highest point. It is located in Taraba state, near the border with Cameroun. 

Olumo Rock

Olumo rock is a massive rock that is located in Ogun state of Nigeria. It has served as a tourist attraction to many people from around the world. 

Zuma Rock

This rock is located in Niger state. It is visible from Abuja along the main road to Niger state. It is as high as 700 metres. 

Rivers in Nigeria

Rivers are natural flow of water that continues in a long line across land and empties into seas or oceans. Nigeria has many rivers and the two biggest rivers are River Niger and River Benue. Famous rivers in Nigeria include Ogun river, Yobe river, Cross-River, Anambra river, etc. 

Did you know?

"...that  the point where two rivers meet is called a Confluence. The Niger-Benue confluence is located in Kogi state."

Kogi state is popularly known as the the Confluence state.

Map of Nigeria showing River flow

Rivers are very important for the growth and development of a community or Nation. There are various uses of rivers to peole all over the country. 

Importance of Rivers
  • 1. Domestic uses like washing and cooking
  • 2. As a food source - Fishermen in places like Port-harcourt , cross-river and Bayelsa rely greatly on fish and other sea food.
  • 3. Transportation - the use of ships, boats, ferries make use of rivers to move from one place to another.
  • 4. Irrigation - Farmers use water from rivers to water their plants. 
    5. Electricity - The use of dams in rivers converts energy from moving water to spin mechanical devices that generate electricity. An example is the Kainji dam that uses the river Niger to generate electricity.
