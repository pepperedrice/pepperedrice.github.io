Cash crops and Food Crops

Cash Crops are plants that farmers grow mainly to sell either in the local market or to export to foreign countries. They are usually sold to factories in large quantities to be processed into manufactured goods such as clothes, soaps, canned foods, car tyres and many more. Factories in Nigeria that are unable to process these cash crops are sold to other countries as exports. Examples of export cash crops are cocoa, cotton, rubber, coffee beans, and tobacco. 

Cocoa pods

Cocoa is a cash crop used to produce chocolate and bevrages like milo and bournvita. Cocoa is grown in Ogun state.

Cotton

Kastina state is popular for growing cotton. It is used in the production of cotton fabric that is used in making clothes. Cotton can be dyed to many different colours.

Oil palm

Oil palm is used in the production of palm oul (red oil) and materials for local soap. Oil palm is also used for cooking local dishes. It is grown in Enugu state. 

Tobacco

This cash crop is used to produce tobacco cigarettes and pesticides.

Rubber sap

The bowl is tied to the tree to extract rubber sap from the tree. Rubber is popular i Edo state. It is used to produce materials used in making rubber tyres and cotainers. 

Groundnuts

Ground nuts is grown in Kano. It is used in making vegetable oil and margarine. Ground nut is also a food crop. 

Agriculture is one of Nigeria's main source of revenue. It also creates jobs for people. Other examples of cash crops in Nigeria include Oil palm, groundnuts, ginger cassava, yam, rice and millet.

Food Crops

Food  crops are plants which farmers grow for consumption and possible to sell if there is any remaining.  

Examples of Food crops

There are many types of food crops grown in different parts of the country. Climate regions make some favorable conditions for some crops to grow and thrive in sub regions of Nigeria. 

  • 1. Northern Region 

    • The northern region of Nigeria provides favorable conditions to grow food crops like rice, maize, guinea corn, yam

  • 2. Southern Region 

    • Food crops like Potatoes, Guinea corn, Maize, millet, plantain, banana etc, can be found in the southern region of Nigeria.
