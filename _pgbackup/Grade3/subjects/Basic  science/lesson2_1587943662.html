Basic Science

Animals

Lesson 2

Wild animals

An animal is a living organism that have adapted to their environment to eat, move and reproduce. Animals are found all over the world in different shapes and sizes.

Classification of Animals

We can group animals using different categories. 

1. The Skeleton
The Skeleton is the frame of animal. Animals that have skeletons are called Vertebrates while animals without skeletons are called invertebrates.
2. The way animals are born. 
Some animals give birth to their young ones alive, these are called mammals. Other animals give birth through eggs(oviparous).
3. The way animals eat
Various animals have adapted to the kind of food around them. There are different types of animals in this category. Herbivores, Carnivores, Omnivores.
4. The way animals move around
Animals move around in various ways. You can observe animals in their animals and notice that not all animals move around in the same way. Animals move around by; Walking, Swimming, Flying and Crawling.
5. Where they are found.
Wild animals are those living organisms found in the forest or in the wild. they are very dangerous. Domestic animals are found in the local environment. They are not as dangerous as wild animals and they can be used as pets.


In order to survive and reproduce, an organism, in this case, an animal, requires nutrition, Oxygen, a habitat and the right temperature.  

You can find all sorts of animals co-existing in a good habitat

Basic Requirements for Animal survival

Water is very important to all living things. All chemical reactions that happen in your body need water. An animal could die if it loses one-tenth of the water in its body. Water also helps in excretion of waste, regulating body temperature and transporting food.

Nitrition is gotten through food, which is different for each animal. Carnivorous animals only eat meat and bones. Herbivorous animals can only eat leaves and plants and Omnivorous animals can eat both meat and grass or vegetables.

All animals must breathe in and breathe out. This action is called respiration. Animals need Oxygen to survive. Land animals breathe in oxygen in the air and marine animals filter oxygen by using their gills. Oxygen also helps in some chemical reactions in the body. 

External temperature is a major factor in animal survival. Some animals can regulate their own body temperature. These animals are called warm blooded animals. While other animals that cannot control their body temperature are called cold blooded animals. Animals exhibit certain behaviour when there is a change in temperature. for example, ears love to sleep during winter when its very cold

Each animal needs a place to sleep. The environment where we can find animals is called their habitat. When an animal finds a good place where it can easily find water, oxygen and food, it will make that place its home. 

A good habitat also provides shelter from the elements of weather, protection from predators and a place to raise their young. Some examples of habitats include; Oceans, Rain forests, the desert, savannahs, etc.
