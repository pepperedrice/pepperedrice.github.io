Sound Energy

Energy is the ability to do work. 

Sound is a type of energy made by vibrations. When any object makes a sound, it causes movement in the air particles. These sound vibrations reach our outer ear and the brain for interpretation.

Sound energy always originates from a point and moves outwards by vibrating other air particles until it runs out of energy. That is why as you move away from a source of sound, the volume of that sound reduces. 

Did you know? 

"that sound  can also vibrate through water, wood, metal and many other objects"

How do we hear sounds?

Our sense organ is the Ear. The outer ear picks up sound vibrations in the air and our ear drums vibrate according to the rhythms of these vibrations. Our brain receives these vibration signals through connective nervs and makes sense out of it.

Sounds vibrate in different frequencies. In music, we have sound properties like pitch and tones.

Uses of sound energy

Sound energy is a very important component of modern technology. Listed below are some of the applications of sound energy in everyday life. 

  • 1. Hospitals use sound waves to see babies in pregnant women
  • 2. Submarines use sound energy to navigate the depths of the sea
  • 3. Some animals use sound vibrations to move around their environment like dolphins and bats
    • 4. Communication devices like phones and laptops and loud speakers
    • 5. Hearing aids
    • 6. Music and television entertainment
    • 7. Religious gathering and public occasions
