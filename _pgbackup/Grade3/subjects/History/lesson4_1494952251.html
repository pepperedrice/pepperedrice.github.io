History

Major Ethnic groups 

Hausa, Igbo and Yoruba

Language Map of Nigeria

Nigeria is the most populous country in Africa and accounts for over half of West Africa's population. The most populous ethnic groups are Hausa and Fulani, Yoruba, and Igbo. These ethnic groups are regarded as Nigeria's major ethnic groups. 

English is the official language spoken in Nigeria. there are over 500 additional indigenous languages. 

Hausa and Fulani

According to Hausa oral traditions, a long time ago, there was a prince called Bayajidda. He had a quarrel with his father, the King Abdullahi of Baghdad and left the palace in anger. Bayajidda travelled east towars Africa and arrived at a place called Daura.

Daura is located in  Katsina state in northern Nigeria.

Did you know?

"... that the Hausa and Fulani ethnic group  accounts for 29% of Nigeria's total population"

After arriving in Daura, Bayagidda met a situation in the community. Something was tormenting the people of Daura and they were in need of deliverance. He soon discovered that there was a big snake in the well where people usually fetched water and no one was brave enough to draw water from the well. The snake was named 'Sarki' which means 'King'.

Bayajidda

Sarki the snake

Bayajidda slays sarki

Bayajidda was brave and he went to the well and fought the snake and killed it. The people of Daura were happy and celebrated their new hero. The Queen of the land was very pleased and decided to marry Bayajidda. They soon had a son and named him 'Bawo'. 

Bawo grew up in Daura and had seven sons. These sons expanded the hausa land to seven small states called the 'Hausa Bakwai'. They are Daura, Biram, Katsina, Zaria, Kano, Rano and Gobir. 

But Bawo didn't stop there, he got really busy and had 7 more children with another wife. These second set of children were called 'Banza Bakwai'. They are Zamfara, Kebbi, Gwari, Yaoi, Nupe, Illorin and Kwararafa. 

The hausa ethnic group started from Daura and expanded throughout the northern region of Nigeria. 

Yoruba 

The yoruba people inhabits the major part of western Nigeria. There are about 44 million people in total. 

According to yoruba oral traditions, the Yoruba people are said to be the descedants of Oduduwa. He was sent by heaven and he arrived in Ile-ife in Osun state with his supporters. The African peoples who lived in the Yorubaland were not initially known as the Yoruba, although they shared their cluture and language. Oduduwa helped to establish a powerful kingdom in Ile-ife, one of the earliest kingdoms in Africa. 

Oduduwa

Regarded as the Spiritual father of the yoruba people

Bronze head statue of Oduduwa in Ile-ife, Oyo state.

Oduduwa coming down from heaven with a gorge of earth to  create Ile-ife

Oduduwa, his sons and grand-sons founded the yoruba states. It is told in traditional stories and legends that Ile-ife is the center of civilization and even the whole world. Oduduwa lived in Ile-ife and it therefore became the spiritual home of the yoruba people.

Another tradition says that Oranmiya, the youngest son of Oduduwa founded Oyo state which became the most powerful Yoruba state. 

Igbo 

The Igbo or Ibo tribe is one of the biggest and most influential ethnic group in Nigeria. Geographcally, the Igbo land is sub-divided into states that cover the south-eastern part of Nigeria. Igbo people speak the igbo language and they work mostly as craftsmen, farmers and traders. 

According to several oral traditions, Eri was a descendant of Jacob the Israelite. Eri was the fifth son of Gad, who was the seventh son of Jacob. He migrated from Egypt with a group of companions many centuries ago and settled in a place called 'Nri' which is in Anambra state.

Eri

Eri, the spiritual father and founder of the Nri igbo kingdom.

Nri kingdom

The Nri infuence in Igboland may be over 600 years old.

Igbo people in their traditional costume

The Nri people of Igbo land also have another creation myth. Many past kings of the igbo land have traced their origins to Eri who was described as a "Sky-being" sent by Chukwu(God). He was believed to have come down to Nri land to give orders, rule and expand the igbo land.

The origins of the igbo people are quite unclear, but one ccan definitly say that the igbo people have an ancient and developed ethnic group.
