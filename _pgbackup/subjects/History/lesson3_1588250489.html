The Emperor of Mali

Lesson 3

Political Map of Bamako, Mali

The land of Mali was once home to some of the great African Empires. Ghana empire was the first to emerge around 700AD. The French colonies came from Europe to colonize the area in the late 1800.

French colonies controlled the area until 1960 when the Sudanese Republic and Senegal formed the Mali Federation and became independent from France. After a few months, Senegal withdrew from the federation and Mali became its own independent country.

Mali is a country in West Africa and its capital city is Bamako. The land area is mostly flat and sandy because the climate is hot and dry. Rainy season only brings little rain to the country. 

The river Niger runs through the middle of south-western Mali. Many countries in West Africa are connected through the river Niger. 

Did you know?

"... that the official Language spoken in Mali is French but they also speak other languages. "

Independence day is September 22nd, 1960.

Mansa Musa: The Emperor of Mali

Mansa Musa was the ruler of the Mali Empire in West Africa from 1312  to 1337, which was about 683 years ago. He controlled territories rich in gold and copper. 'Mansa' was the traditional Mali title meaning 'King'.

Mansa Musa was a muslim by religion. He always travelled on pilgrimage to Mecca and then returned with scholars and architects to build amazing structures and houses in Mali. His tales of fabulous wealth would spread as the Mali Empire's greatest ruler ever. 

He was described as a young man with brown skin, a pleasant face and good figure. His gifts amazed the eyes with their beauty and splendor. If he were to be alive today, he would by far be the richest man alive! 

On one of his travels to Mecca, he passed through the Land of Egypt and as a sign of generosity, he presented gifts to the Sultan of Egypt. It was on record that he gave 50,000 gold bars and other gifts to Egypt. This gift was so much that gold was rendered invaluable and the market could not recover until after 12 years. 

He travelled on many conquests and pilgrimage to holy cities and was inspired by foreign culture and especially education. Mansa Musa brought back scholars and books to educate himself and those around him. He also built mosques and many religious schools. 

The reign of Mansa Musa's empire lasted from 1307 - 1332 (25 years). After his death, the Mali empire began to slowly decline due to the invasion of the French and the Portugese. Other mansas took over the empire but could not run the empire like Musa did. They were always fighting and wanted all the gold and riches to themselves.
