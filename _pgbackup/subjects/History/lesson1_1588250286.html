Nationalism 

Nationalism is an idealogy that promotes the interests of a particular nation. Nigerian Nationalists supported and organised movements aimed at gaining and maintaining control over the nation's government. Nationalist leaders fight for the independence and freedom of their country from their colonial masters. 

Sir Ahmadu Bello

Sir Ahmadu Bello, the Sarudauna is Sokoto, was a great Nigerian and a politician. He was also the first and only Premier of the Northern region of Nigeria.

Did you know?

"... that a Sardauna of Sokoto is regarded as a king. It is the highest honor given to a respected person. 

Sir Ahmadu Bello fought hard for the independence of Nigeria. He loved education so much that he built a university in Zaria which is named after him. 

He was famous for using his royal position to influence the politics of the British colony to ensure the Independence of Nigeria. His portrait is on the 200 Naira note. 

Other Famous Nationalist Leaders include Dr Nnamdi Azikiwe, Abubakar Tafawa Balewa, Magaret Ekpo.

Chief Magaret Ekpo

Magaret Ekpo was a Nigerian Women's right activist and a politician. She would organize rallies and motivated women to speak up for their rights and contribute to the governance of the country. 

Chief Magaret Ekpo joined forces with Dr Nnamdi Azikiwe and led the women's division in his Political party. She used this platform to organize the Aba market women's association in the city of Aba in Abia state. 

After Nigeria gained independence from the British colony in 1960, she was elected into the parliament known as the National Assembly.
