National Values

National Symbols

Lesson 1

National symbols are signs that reflect our identity as a nation anywhere in the world.

National symbols are very important to the existence of our nation. They must be preserved and respected. 

Exampls of our national symbols are - The Nigerian Coat of arms, the National Flag, The Mace, the Nigerian Constitution, the Nigerian Passport, the Nigerian Currency.

The Nigerian Constitution

The Nigerian Constitution is an important national symbol. It is the supreme law of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

The Constitution is a document that contains established laws that guides a people. It contains guidelines on how people should behave in a country and also states the punishment for disobeying laws. The Nigerian constitutionprotects its citizens.

There is only one constitution for Nigeria as a country. New laws are added and old ones are taken out of the constitution by elected leaders of the country. These leaders are called Senators.

Did you know?

"...that the process of adding and removing laws from the constitution is called an Amendment."

All the elected leaders that amend laws in the constitution cather in a building called the National Assembly.

The Nigerian Currency

The national currency is a system of money used to trade in a particular country. The currency we use in Nigeria is called Naira and Kobo. It is the money that has been accepted for buying and selling within the nigerian borders. 

Naira is made up of many small denominations. Each denomination is either a note or a coin that has a number value printed on it. They all carry portraits of our national heroes and other historical moments.

Naira and kobo are symbols of national unity in Nigeria. It also represents the economic dimension of civic education.

The Nigerian Passport

The Nigerian passport is also called the International passport. It is a small green booklet that identifies you as a citizen of Nigeria when you visit another country. 

It contains all your necessary information and it is always presented and stamped when entering the borders or territory of another country. 

The Nigerian passport is green in colour and it can be used to travel easily across West Africa. Countries in the West Africa use the same color of passport. They belong to a community called ECOWAS which stands for Economic Community Of West African States. The international passport is given to every citizen by the Nigerian Immigration Service.
