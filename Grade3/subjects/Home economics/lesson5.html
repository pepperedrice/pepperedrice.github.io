Lesson 5

Food items are classified or grouped based on the nutrients they contain.

There is a real relationship between classes of food and the functions they perform in the body. As we grow older and stronger, our bodies need specific nutrients to keep us healthy. 

Food is classified or grouped into - Energy giving, Body building, Disease fighting and Thirst relieving foods.

A diet that has proper proportions of carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins, minerals and water is called Balanced diet.

Classes of foods 

Food items are classified into groups based on the nutrients they contain. These nutrients perform specific functions in your body. These nutrients include - Carbohydrates, Protein, Vitamins, Fat, Minerals and Water. 

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrate is essential for producing energy. The brain and the body will not function well without this nutrient. Carbohydrate is an energy giving nutrient and it can be found in foot items like - rice, maize, wheat, corn, bread, yam, cassava, etc. Eating these foot items provide the necessary energy to perform daily activities. 

Protein

Foods that contain protein supply energy for the body to grow. It is the main component that forms the body. The food a child eats must have enough protein because a child's body grows at a much faster rate than a grown up.

Some sources of protein are - chicken, beef, fish, milk, beans and eggs. 

Protein is needed for good hair, skin, eyes and strong muscles.

Vitamins

Vitamins are the essential nutrients that protects your body from diseases. They boost your immune system and give your body the energy it needs to fight of diseases that make you sick. 

There are different types of vitamins that perform different functions in your body. Examples of vitamins are - Vitamin A, B, C, D, E and K. 

Some natural sources of vitamins are fruits and vegetables. The Sun is a good source of vitamin D which helps protect the skin.

Fat

Fat helps to absorb vitamins into the body. Vitamins such as A,D,E and K.

Fat is one nutrient that behaves like many other nutrients. They give energy and help to develop the brain and bones. 

Some sources of fat include vegetable oils, avocado, nuts and seeds. Milk, meat and fish are also rich sources of fat as well.

Minerals

Minerals are chemicals required as an essential nutrient by living organism to perform functions that are necessary for life. The body needs minerals like calcium, iron, potassium and many others. 

Our body needs calcium to maintain healthy bones and teeth. Calcuim is gotten from milk. Iron is also a mineral which helps in the formation of blood. We can get iron by eating foods like - red meat, fruits and green leafy vegetables. Potassium can be gotten from banana.

Water

Water is basically the most important nutrient of all. No living organism can survive without water. It builds the transportation system of the body. Water carries nutrients from one part of the body to another.

Water helps to remove waste products from the body. Driking clean water helps in digestion and can help your brain to perform its necessary daily functions. 

Along with bottled and tap water, other sources of water include - soup, watermelon, cucumber and some juicy fruits. Please make sure to drink enough water everyday to stay healthy and strong.
