Constitutional Authority

Lesson 3

Constitutional authority refers to a person or a group of people that are appointed to control the affairs of a community or country.

The authority given to these people are supported by the law. They help to make decisions on behalf of the people. They also uphold and protect the laws that govern people.

Any member of a constituted authority is not above the law, so they also must be held accountable for their actions. 

Did you know?

"...that in the school, the constituted authority figures are - Head teacher, Administrators, Teachers, class monitors, etc.  

There are many examples of authority figures in the community, they include - Police, firefighters, soldiers, judges, pastors, etc.

Types of Constituted Authority
1. Religious leaders

They teach us to uphold good morlas in our respective faiths. Their duty is to lead us to worship God. Examples of religious leaders are Pastors, Imams, Priests, Nuns, etc. 

2. Political leaders

Political leaders are a group of people elected by the community to lead and guide them in the affairs of the government. Examples are - President, Senators, Governors, Local government chairmen.

3. Traditional leaders

These people are chosen by the community according to the traditions of the people. Examples are  - Oba, Obi, Eze, Sultan, Chief, Obong, Emirs, Kings, etc. 

4. Heads of organizations

These figures are in charge of government institutions  and private organizations like schools, banks, Ministries and government offices. 

5. Professionals

Professionals are people that are experts that are trained and have enough experience to manage their occupations. Examples are Lawyers, accountants, bankers, doctors, teachers, architects, etc.

5. The Military and Law enforcers

These authority figures have been employed by the government to enforce the law and defend the borders of the country. Examples are - Police, Army, Navy, Airforce, Road safety, Traffic warden, etc. 

Duties of Constitutional authority
  • 1. To maintain law and order in the community
  • 2. To defend people from enemies
  • 3. To promote the welfare and standards of living of citizens
  • 4. Lead people well in honesty and transparency
  • 5. To protect and uphold the rights of members of the community
