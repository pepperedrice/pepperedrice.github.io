The Skeleton

All the bones in the body together are called the Skeletal system. It provides strength and rigidity to our body so we don't flop around like jellyfish. Each bone has a function. An adult has 206 bones in the body. Animals with skeletons are called Vertebrates, meaning they have a spine. 

Bones are made up of a hard outer covering, spongy bone tissue and the bone marrow with is the tissue found in the hollow part of bones.

A broken bone is called a Fracture and X-rays are used to see broken bones in the body.

Did you know that a baby is born with 300 bones. These bones gradually fuse together to form a total of 206 bones that adults have. 

For healthy and strong bones, always eat foods that contain calcium, maintain a good walking and sitting posture and use protective equipment when working or playing

Types of Bones in the skeleton
  • Long Bones - Leg bones, Arm bnes, Fingers and toes
  • Short bones - Wrist, ankles, Knee cap
  • Flat Bones - Ribs, Skull, Pelvis
  • Irregular bones - Bones in the spine (vertebrae), Bones of the face
    • Bones come together to form joints

    Joints help the bones to move in different diections. Some type of joints are:

    • Ball and socket joints
      These joints help bones to move in many directions. They are found in the shoulders and hips
    • Hinge joints
      • Hinge joints are found in the elbows, knees, finger and toes. They allow bones to move back and forth in one direction. 
    • Pivot joints
      • This joint is found in the neck. It helps the head to turn side to side without turning the whole body
    • Fixed joints
      • A fixed joint doesnt allow movement. They are locked together. It is found in the skull. 

      Functions of the Skeleton

    • Helps you to move around
    • It gives your body its shape
    • Your spine helps to keep your body up
    • Special bones help protect vital organs in your body
    • Red blood cells are produced in the bone marrow
