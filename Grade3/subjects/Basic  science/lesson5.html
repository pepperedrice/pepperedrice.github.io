Rocks

Lesson 5

A Rock is a solid made up of a bunch of different minerals

A rock is a solid material that is made up combinations of different minerals over a long period of time. Rocks can be in any size. Some are smaller than a grain of sand while others can be as large as Zuma rock. 

Rocks are everywhere! You have definitely seen rocks before. This lesson will teach you about the different properties and types of rocks. Scientists generally classify rocks by how they were made or formed. 

There are three major types of rocks - Metamorphic, Igneous and Sedimentary rocks.  

Did you know?

"... that some rocks actually come from space. They are called Meteorites.

Types of Rocks

Earth is made up of various living and non living materials. Elements make up minerals and minerals form rocks. 

Igneous Rocks

Igneous rocks are formed from volcanoes. When a volcano erupts, it spews out hot molten rock called Magma or Lava. Eventually, the magma will cool down and harden. The magma can cool either on the earth's surface where it can be visible, or under the earth's surface. Some molten lava cool down under the ocean. This hardened magma is called Igneous rock. 

Volcanic eruption

As pieces of rock compress under the earth's surface, pressure and temperature builds up in a volcano and red hot magma shoots out of the mouth of the volcano.

Magma

Very hot liquid rock called magma flows out of the volcano and slowly cools to form solid igneous rocks. 

Igneous rocks

Various types of minerals come together to form igneous rocks of  different properties. Examples of igneous rocks are: Granite, Basalt, Diorite. 

Sedimentary rocks

Sedimentary rocks are formed from years and years of sediments compacting together and becoming hard. Generally, sediments are smaller particles of lose rocks that can be carried around by wind or water. Something like a stream of water can carry lots of small pieces of rocks and minerals to a larger body of water. These pieces will settle at the bottom and over a long time, they will compress and form into solid rock. 

Sedementary rock formation

Sediments are different particles, rocks and minerals that are washed away by erosion to the bottom of the ocean.

Layers of rocks

Simply because of they way it is formed, sedimentary rocks form layers of rocks in coorful patterns. Archieologists use sedimentary rocks to study history.

Examples

Sedimentary rocks are rich in minerals that are useful to man. Some examples of sedimentary rocks are - Limestone, Conglomerate, sandstone, siltstone, etc. 

Metamorphic rocks

These rocks can also be known as 'Changing rocks'. Metamorphic rocks are formed by great heat and pressure. They are usually derived from either igneous rocks or sedimentary rocks that have changed in composition, texture and their internal structure. 

These types of rock is a rock is a result of changing an original rock using extreme heat and high pressure. 

Metamorphic rock formation

For example, shale is a type of sedimentary rock. Under heat and pressure, it changes to slate and finally into Gnesis.

Layers of rocks under pressure

The layers in this metamorphic rocks have been compacted inwards because of the high pressure.

Examples

Metamorphic rocks are used for  decorations. Examples are - Quarzite, Marble, Slate, Gnesis, etc. Marble is formed from limestone.

Properties of rocks.

A property is a way of describing how something looks or feels. Rocks have different properties. Some of which are: 

  • Colour 
  • Texture - How the rock feels when you touch it
  • Size - How big the rock is
  • Density - How heavy a rock is compared to its size
  • Composition - What a rock is made up of
Uses of Rocks
  • 1. Rocks like granite are used to make foundations of buildings
  • 2. Rocks are used to make railway balast
  • 3. They are a valuable source of minerals like Gold, marble, precious stones. 
  • 4. Limestone is the basic material used to manufacture cement
  • 5. Rocks are used as blocks along river dams to reduce erosion and prevent flooding
  • 6. Some rocks are toursts attractions like Olumo rock.
  • 7. They are used in the construction of Roads and dams
  • 8. Rocks can be used for decoration like marbles
