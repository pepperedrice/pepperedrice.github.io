The Gold Coast

The Republic of Ghana is a small country on the west coast of Africa. Its southern border touches the Atlantic ocean. Accra is the capital city and her citizens are called Ghanians.

Ghana wasnt always the country's name. When the area was still a British colony, it was called the Gold coast. People and traders from all over Europe would come to the colony to trade goods such as food supplies, jewelries and slaves. Ghana had a lot of gold, that is why they were called the gold coast. 

Did you know?

"... that in 1957, the Gold coast  gained independence from Britain and changed its name to Ghana. "

Ghana is a multi-lingual country in which about eighty languages are spoken. Of all the languages, English is the official language spoken in Ghana. There are as many as 75 different ethnic groups found in Ghana. 

The largest ethnic group is the Akan tribe, which makes up almost half of the country's population. 

Traditional clothing is very colourful. The 'Kente' is a woven cotton and silk cloth with many colours and prints.

The British - Ashanti War

The British - Ashanti war was a series of five battles that took place between the year 1824 and 1990. The Ashanti Empire in the interior part of the Gold coast fought against European colones in different attempts to defend their land against British soldiers who came to take possession.

The Battle

The Fall of the Ashanti Empire

The British Royal Army arrived at the Gold coast of West Africa and established a military base. Many Europeans were very interested in the minerals found in the gold coast. Europeans like Germans, Swiss and the French. 

During many invasions of the British army, they advanced at a disadvantage because the Ashanti kingdom was located in the interior part of West Africa. The British couldn't fight because they were falling sick due to the environment. 

The British - Ashanti war continued for many years and both sides suffered many casualties. British armies used sophisticated weapons and gained the upper hand in the battle and took possesion of some of the land. 

War of the Golden stool

The Fifth Ashanti war is popurlarly known as the war of the golden stool. It all began in the year 1990 when a representative of the British army in the fallen empire made a political error by insisting that he wanted to sit on a golden stool. 

The British officer, Sir Frederick Mitchell Hodgson did not understand that the golden stool was a sacred throne to the Ashanti Empire. He demanded that the chair be brought to him so he could sit on it. All the elders in the empire refused to give him the stool and they decided to hide it. Sir Frederick was upset and orderded his men to search for it. 

The soldiers under orders turned the empire upside down in search of the stool, but the people soon became angry and attacked all the soldiers engaged in the search. 

After a while, the Ashanti kingdom and the British Colony reached a truce in order to avoid a war due to misunderstanding. No Ashanti Soldiers were killed in this war.
