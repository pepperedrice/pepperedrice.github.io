Lesson 2

Weather

Weather is the condition of the atmosphere of a place at a particular time. The earth is so big that it has different weather conditions of different places at the same time. Seasons are times during the year with different types of weather. These seasons are Summer, Winter, Autumn and Spring. 
In some places, the weather is always warm or sometimes hot because of direct exposure to sunlight. These places are called Tropics. Examples of tropic areas are West Africa, South America.

Other places that experience constant cool temperatures and snow are called temperate regions. Some really cool places are Russia, Europe, Canada, Alaska. 

How weather affects our daily life

Most activities of man are influenced by the weather condition at that particular time. The food we eat in a season, the clothes we wear, the type of houses we live in, our travel destinations, even the type of sports we play are affected by the weather. 

Climate is the total average of weather conditions of a place over a long period of time. Nigeria has a tropical or warm climate. Rainfall determines the level of temperature of most places. 
Nigeria's Weather and Vegetation

Nigeria has two main weather conditions. Rainy season and dry season. 

The climate map above shows the weather and climate distribution in Nigeria. The Monsoon climate areas are the costal areas like Calabar, Port-harcourt, Bayelsa and Lagos are colder because winds blow in cold air from the Atlantic ocean. These areas are mostly swampy because of the long rainy seasons. 

The Tropical savanna climate is popularly known as the middle-belt of Nigeria. This area experiences moderate rainfall through out the year. Places like Sokoto, Kaduna, Borno, Kano are hotter than most places in Nigeria. Hot binds brin in hot air from the sahara desert. As a result, those areas experience little rain fall through out the year.

Vegetation

Natural vegetation in Nigeria refers to all the plants that grow in Nigeria. Vegetation in Nigeria is determined by the amount of rainfall per year. That is why vegetation is different in some parts of Nigeria.

There are two main types of vegetation in Nigeria. 

  • 1. Forest Area - Swamp forest and Rain forest
  • 2. Savannah (grass land) - Guinea, Sudan and Sahel Savannah

    • Swamp 

    The Swamp forest has a swampy land that is always filled with water. This swamp forest is not suitable for farming.

    Rain Forest

    The Rain forest has tall and very big trees. Plants used for food and medicines can be found here. The rain forest is very important in Nigeria. 

    Savannah

    The savannah is mainly grassland and has a few trees, not as many as the swamp or rain forest. This area of land is good for rearing cattle and growing crops like corn and millet.
