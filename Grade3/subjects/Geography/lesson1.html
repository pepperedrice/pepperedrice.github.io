What is Geography?

Geography is the study of the earth and everything in it. We learn practical geography by observing and studying the world around us. the earth is very huge and it contains many different things in it. 

Tools used in geography

In basic science, we have learned about instruments used in taking accurate measurements. These tools are used to take accurate measurement of the earth. 

Maps

Previous Next

Maps are drawings of different places. They are representation of the earth surfaces in different details. A map can help is to find directions to a specific location when we are lost.

Globe

Previous Next

A globe is a spehrical ball with the map of the earth on it. It is a model of the earth. It shows the shapes, sizes and location of the earth's land and water. 

Compass

Previous Next

A compass is an instrument that helps us to find our direction. It has a pin that always points to the north pole of the earth. 

Other Geographical tools include computers, satellites, GPS, Wind vanes. All of the earth's natural features like mountains, oceans, lakes are geographical tools that help us to find our our way around the big planet earth which we live in. 

Importance of geography
  • Geography helps us to study the earth's features like mountains, oceans, rivers, valleys, weather and climates.
  • It helps us to use our environment to find our direction so we don't get lost.
  • Geography helps us to discover the activities of ethnic groups in different locations and weather conditions.
  • The study of geography can help us to reshape the future.
How to read a map

Maps have signs and symbols that are used to represent both natural and artificial features in an area. We use these signs to read the map for better understanding of the environment as well as names of cities, rivers, buildings and institutions. 

There are different types of maps.

1.  Political Maps - A political map shoes the state and national boundaries of a place. It shows the location of cities with respect to each other

2. Physical Maps - A physical map is one which shows the physical features of a place or country like rivers, mountains and forests. The physical features are shown in different colours. Rivers are shown in blue, forests in green and some mountains are shown in brown. 

3. Road Maps - This map shows different roads, highways or railways present in an area. It is usually very detailed and is generally used for direction purposes.

Are maps really that useful?

Yes they are! Maps are useful to us because they help us to understand roads and directions to new places.

Maps also help us to calculate and estimate the distance between places, heights of mountains, depths of rivers and valleys. The government also need maps to take record of the city during plannind and when citizens buy lands and houses.

Cardinal Points

Cardinal points are used to show the directions of places. There are eight cardinal points. North, North-East, East, South-East, South, South-West, West, North-West. 

A compass is an instrument used to find the north direction on a map. A good geography student learns how to use a map, cardinal points and a compass to find the directions to any place.
